What we do

We build stuff. Could've sworn I mentioned that...

You have a restaurant? A band? A budding side-gig? You probably need a web site. We can help.

You have an idea for a mobile app, but you don't know how to make them? We do! We can help.

You have an idea for a product—a new design on the toothpick, or an electronic sock, maybe—but you don't know how to make plastic things or circuits? We can help.

At Martes Delta Co, we've been doing "this stuff" for about 20 years—from designing and/or building web sites and web applications to designing and building mobile applications and games to prototyping electronics circuits—for everyone from mom and pop to Fortune 50 companies.

Services we provide:

  • Simple web sites
  • Web applications
  • iOS & Android Apps
  • Consulting on any of the above
  • Product prototyping

"I'm using Squarespace"

Great! Squarespace (and Weebly, Shopify, WordPress, and the like) is a great platform, but you'll still need an idea of how your site should look and work, and the initial setup can still be daunting. We're happy to help with that sort of thing, too.

Who we are

Thomas Q Brady, owner

Thomas has been working in software design and development for about 20 years, having worked for companies like Visa, Polycom, Zynga, H-E-B, Dell, and frog, as well as at some startups and incubators you may not have heard of. He's been a "design technologist" (someone who straddles the fence between design and engineering, often working right at that intersection by building prototypes or doing other tasks that require both skillsets) for most of his career, but has also had titles like UX designer, interaction designer, front-end engineer, and he was even a technical writer at one point.

What things cost

We'll level with you—we're still figuring that out. Martes Delta Co aims to be a sliding-scale sort of an outfit in the long-run. We would love to have a model wherein we can do really cool shit with people who can afford that, but still help the restaurant around the corner that wants a good online ordering experience and thinks they can't afford it, either by simplifying until it's affordable enough, or even doing some occasional pro bono work.
